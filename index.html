Data Engineering

Data Cleaning Application

I developed a Data Cleaning App designed to automate the most tedious parts of data preparation. The application supports various file formats including CSV, Excel, and even ZIP folders for bulk processing. One of the core features is an Automatic Duplicate Check, which I demonstrated by identifying 320 duplicates in a 500-row set. After cleaning, the tool produced a high-quality dataset of 180 unique rows, ensuring data integrity. The app includes a KPI Builder and manual duplicate removal options to give users full control over their data. By integrating text transformation filters, this tool significantly reduces the time spent on manual data entry and cleaning. This project showcases my ability to bridge the gap between software development and data analysis.

Case Studies

Swiggy Sales Analysis

I built a detailed Sales Analysis dashboard for Swiggy, processing a massive 52.12M in total sales. The analysis tracked 193.78K total orders with an average order price of 268.97 and an average rating of 4.34. I identified Bengaluru as the top city by revenue (4.6M) and analyzed food types to find that Non-Veg sales account for 64.48% of total income. The dashboard highlights the "Best Selling Dishes" and provides a "Poor Performing Restaurant" list to help managers improve service. By visualizing weekly sales trends, the project offers a clear window into daily demand fluctuations. This dashboard serves as a vital tool for understanding regional market dominance and customer preference.

Amazon Store Sales

For the Amazon project, I developed a Store Sales Dashboard tracking 2M in total sales and 175K in profit. I analyzed consumer behavior across segments, finding that the Consumer category accounts for 48% of the total business. The data revealed that Cash on Delivery (43%) and Online payments (35%) are the leading payment methods. I also mapped sales geographically across North America and tracked returns, showing a high 95.14% acceptance rate. Technology sub-categories like Phones were identified as the highest profit drivers, contributing 0.20M to sales. This project masterfully uses color-coding and trend lines to provide a 360-degree view of retail performance.
